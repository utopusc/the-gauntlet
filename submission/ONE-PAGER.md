# THE GAUNTLET — One-Pager

**The fundraising flight simulator. Beat 3 AI investor bosses, walk out with a fundability verdict.**

GDG Stanford × Red Bull Basement — THE GAUNTLET. Powered by Google Gemini.

---

## The Team
A founder-engineer duo who have sat on both sides of the table — pitched VCs, been told "great idea, come back in 6 months," and watched the *real* reason (no defensible moat, no GTM math, no idea what their CAC is) go unsaid in the room. We built the brutally honest practice partner we wish we'd had before walking into Sand Hill Road.

## The Problem
**Most founders fail the pitch before they ever build the product — and nobody tells them why.**
- First-time founders get one real shot per investor. They burn it raw.
- VC "no"s are diplomatic, vague, and unactionable ("interesting, keep us posted").
- Accelerator mentor hours are scarce, scheduled weeks out, and expensive.
- The real questions — *What's your wedge? Why now? What breaks at scale? What's your CAC payback?* — only surface live, under pressure, when it's too late to prep.

There is no low-stakes, on-demand, *honest* place to get punched in the face by the questions that actually decide a round.

## The Solution
**THE GAUNTLET is a Gemini-powered VC boss battle.** A founder types one idea and fights through three AI investor bosses, each a distinct adversarial persona:

1. **The Market Boss** — TAM reality, timing, "why now," competitive moat.
2. **The Tech Boss** — defensibility, scalability, what breaks at 1M users, build-vs-buy.
3. **The GTM Boss** — CAC/LTV, distribution wedge, first 100 customers, go-to-market math.

Each boss has an **HP bar**. Sharp, specific answers drain it; hand-wavy answers cost *you* HP. Gemini scores every exchange in real time on substance, specificity, and defensibility. Survive all three and you unlock a **shareable Term Sheet card** with a fundability score (e.g. **84/100**), strengths, fatal gaps, and the one question you ducked. Lose, and you get the same diagnostic — minus the bragging rights.

It's a fundraising rehearsal disguised as a game. The diagnostic is the product; the game is the wedge.

## Why Now
- **Gemini makes the adversary real.** Long-context reasoning + structured output means each boss can hold your full pitch in memory, attack the *weakest* claim, and emit a clean scored JSON verdict — not a generic chatbot reply. This was not possible at this quality 18 months ago.
- **Founder volume is exploding.** AI lowered the cost of starting a company to near zero; the bottleneck moved from *building* to *being fundable*. Supply of founders is up; quality of preparation is flat.
- **"Shareable verdict" is a proven growth loop.** Score cards (Spotify Wrapped, credit-score apps, personality tests) are 2025's dominant organic-acquisition pattern. A "fundability score" card is inherently competitive and inherently postable.

## Market
- **Beachhead:** pre-seed / first-time founders + accelerator and demo-day cohorts (YC, Techstars, university entrepreneurship programs, GDG/hackathon circuits). Tens of thousands of new founders per year actively seeking practice.
- **Expansion:** B2B licensing to **accelerators, universities, and VC scout programs** as a standardized founder-readiness assessment — turning a viral consumer hook into a screening + curriculum tool.
- **Adjacent:** the same boss-battle engine generalizes to *any* high-stakes conversation rehearsal — sales pitches, exec interviews, board defense, med-school MMIs. The fundraising vertical is the wedge into "adversarial AI rehearsal" as a category.

## Traction (Hackathon Build)
- **Working end-to-end demo:** type an idea → fight a scored boss → watch HP drain → win/lose → generate a shareable Term Sheet card with a numeric verdict. Built in the hackathon window on Gemini.
- **Built-for-virality by construction:** every play produces one postable artifact (the score card). Demo-day audiences become the first distribution test.
- **Honest scope:** scoring is calibrated against a small VC-question rubric, not a claim of investor-grade prediction. We're transparent that v1 is a *rehearsal and diagnostic*, not a verdict on your company.
